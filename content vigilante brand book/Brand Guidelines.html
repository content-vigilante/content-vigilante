01 / FOUNDATION

What we are.

Content Vigilante audits marketing content against your brand guidelines in seconds. Drop in your guide and any piece of work — get a scored report with annotated deviations and suggested rewrites. The owl is the watcher; the system is what makes the work feel calm enough to be trusted.

PILLAR 01

Vigilance, not aggression.

We watch, we flag, we explain. We don't moralise. The owl observes; it never lectures.

PILLAR 02

Verdicts over hedges.

Every audit ends in a clear judgement: on-brand, drift, or off-brand. No "it depends." No four bullets when one will do.

PILLAR 03

Evidence in the margin.

We show our work. Every flag points to a clause in the guide. No black-box scoring.

RULE 01

05 / VOICE & TONE

Quietly authoritative. Never loud.

We are the seasoned editor in the room. Dry, precise, occasionally funny. Verdicts, not opinions. Evidence, not adjectives.

VOICE 01

Plain over clever.

Say what's wrong. Say how to fix it. The cleverness is in being concise — never in being cute.

VOICE 02

Short over long.

Never use three sentences when one will do. Default to fragments where they read clearly.

VOICE 03

Verdicts over hedges.

"Off-brand on tone." Not "may be slightly off-brand depending on interpretation."

VOICE 04

Evidence over opinion.

Every flag cites a clause. Never "I feel" — always "the guide says."

07 / IMAGERY

Workspace photography, never AI gloss.

Real desks, real screens, real proofs. Top-down printed pages with hand annotations. People reading, not posing. Avoid stock cliches: handshakes, lightbulbs, hooded hackers, robot hands.

DO

Natural light. Top-down crops.

Shoot proofs and printouts from above. Keep human hands in frame, soft and out of focus. Use the navy/cream palette for surfaces and props.

DON'T

Stock photography. AI portraits.

No glossy team shots, no rendered "diverse office" composites, no robot hands typing. If we don't have it, we use a placeholder.

08 / COMPLIANCE STAMPS

The verdict, made visible.

Stamps appear on every audit. They are the most public-facing piece of the system — the surface marketers screenshot and share.

On-brand · 92 / 100 Drift · 74 / 100 Off-brand · 38 / 100 Approved EN-IT-FR-DE Pending review Violations: 4
RULE 01

One verdict per artifact.

Never two stamps on the same content. The most severe verdict wins.

RULE 02

Score, then label.

Always pair the stamp with the numeric score. The label without the number is opinion.

RULE 03

Stamp on the artifact.

Burn-in the stamp on exports. Never as a separate file. The verdict travels with the work.

09 / MULTILINGUAL

Four languages, one register.

English, Italian, French, German. The register is identical in all four — direct, formal, second person. Never adapt voice to local quirks; the brand is the same wherever the audit ships.

EN · default

Stop shipping off-brand content.

Source · second person · imperative

IT

Niente più contenuti fuori brand.

Formal · imperative · no inversions

FR

Plus de contenu hors marque.

Vouvoiement · sober · 2-line max

DE

Schluss mit Off-Brand-Content.

Sie-Form · compound nouns OK

RULE

The slogan never gets cute in translation.

It should sound like a directive in every market. If a translation reads as advice, it's wrong.

RULE

The owl, wordmark, and stamps are universal.

Visual identity is identical across all four locales. Only running copy localizes.
